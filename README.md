# 📊 HR Analytics Dashboard

## Overview
This repository contains an interactive Tableau BI dashboard focused on **HR Analytics**.  
The dashboard provides insights into employee attrition, workforce demographics, and job satisfaction trends, enabling HR teams to identify key patterns and make data-driven decisions.

![HR Analytics Dashboard Screenshot](https://github.com/Amit-DataDoc23/HR_Analytics_Report/blob/a1c934718a539441832420a33ba2572b58cb0de9/HR_Analytics_dashboard.png)

## Key Features
- **KPIs at a glance**  
  - Total Employees: 1,470  
  - Attrition Count: 237  
  - Attrition Rate: 16.12%  
  - Active Employees: 1,233  
  - Average Age: 37  

- **Department-wise Attrition**  
  - Sales: 38.82%   
  - R&D:  56.12%
  - HR: 5.06%  

- **Age Group Distribution**  
  Histogram showing employee concentration across age ranges, with peak around age between 32-36.

- **Job Satisfaction Ratings**  
  Tabular breakdown of satisfaction levels (1–4) across roles such as Sales Executive, Research Scientist, Laboratory Technician, and more.

- **Attrition by Gender**  
  - Female: 87  
  - Male: 150  

- **Attrition by Education**  
  Life Sciences, Medical, Marketing, Technical Degree, HR, and Others.

- **Attrition Rate by Gender & Age Group**  
  Circular charts highlighting attrition trends segmented by age brackets (Under 25, 25–34, 35–44, 45–54, Over 55).

## Insights
- R&D department shows the **highest attrition** rate.  
- Employees aged **25–34** contribute most to attrition, especially males.  
- Job satisfaction varies significantly across roles, with Sales Executives and Research Scientists forming the largest groups.  
- Education background plays a role, with **Life Sciences and Medical** fields showing higher attrition counts.

## Tech Stack
- **Tool:** Tableau BI  
- **Data Source:** HR dataset (employee demographics, attrition, job satisfaction, education)  
- **Visuals Used:** KPIs, Pie Charts, Bar Graphs, Tables, Circular Charts
